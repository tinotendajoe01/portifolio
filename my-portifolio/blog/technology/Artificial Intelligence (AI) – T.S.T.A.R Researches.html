With The Great Power Of Artificial Intelligence Comes Great Responsibility!

June 23, 2019June 23, 2019 Tinotenda JoeLeave a comment

Due to the rapid development of
computational power, the capacity of AI to perform new and more complicated tasks is quickly
advancing. And despite of who you ask, AI is known to be the stuff dreams or nightmares. I believe it is definitely the former and I strongly fear that it could also be the latter.

It has become clear that deploying AI requires careful management to prevent unintentional but significant damage, not only to brand reputation but, more important, to workers, individuals, and society as a whole

AI adoption and machine learning are revolutionising industries and societies at large. Their capabilities are embedded in our everyday lives and their applications are expanding. The outcomes of their decisions now impact lives, and with that comes significant risk. For these technologies to continue to have a positive impact on society, they need to abide by humanity’s ethics and principles. From the beginning we know that technology is only as good as its creator, so everyone working in the field is responsible for creating an ethical framework for AI that reflects our values

We are all in this together from the creators,research institutions, business leaders, the local governments to every individual in the society we all have a role to play. Here we are going to focus on the government and the institutions.

THE LOCAL GOVERNMENT

The local government, must always collect information and plan the best approach for its industries. This can be done by the government updating and issuing plans for the individuals to abide. For example the federal government of the USA in 2016, issued the National Artificial Intelligence Research and Development Strategic Plan that outlined seven priorities for federally-funded AI research. These included making long-term investments in AI; developing effective methods for human-AI collaboration; and addressing the ethical, legal, and societal implications of AI. These are some of the issues the governments must address. Additionally, some of the issues they must always address are safety and security, public datasets,standards, and workforce needs.

The Government must always be accountable for technology assessment and issuing reports on challenges,implications and also noting significant potentials for AI to improve many industries. With which today we can see the potentials in finance, transportation, and cybersecurity. They must also note areas where
research is still needed, including how to optimally regulate AI, how to ensure the availability
and use of high quality data, understanding AI’s effects on employment and education, and the
development of computational ethics to guide the decisions made by software.
These are all critical issues the federal government must address.Institutes and even individuals in the field also must consider and abide to the regulations posed by their local governments.

RESEARCH AND LEARNING INSTITUTIONS

When it comes to AI, Technological and Scientific institutions must maintain their number one goal which is research and data sharing contributions among standard developments, regulatory approaches and education.

Today the most important aspect institutions must fuse to their researches is the explainability of AI which could help enhance and establish trust in the behavior of AI systems.

With a helping hand from the local governments in incentivizing data sharing of the data sets that are subject to limitations for how they can be used, research institutions will be able to keep creating frameworks of sharing data among themselves. Improving the safety of AI systems.

These efforts alongside the local governments will include updating the standards for explainability, data labeling and safety, which will include risk management and benchmarking the performance of the systems against the status quo

In Learning Institutions, the education and training has to be reimagined so that workers will have the skills need to work with and along side AI technologies. This is because for us to effectively manage AI systems we need a deeper understanding of probability and statistics across most if not all academic disciplines that is not just the biological sciences, engineering, physical and risk management.

In conclusion, we must always consider the need to grapple with issues regarding the data that we use to “educate” machines, this is because biased data will lead to biased results from seemingly objective machines.

The expected Impact Of AI On The Future Of workforce.(Humans vs AI)

June 21, 2019June 22, 2019 Tinotenda JoeLeave a comment

Last month i posted this question on my Twitter timeline: “ what comes to your mind when you hear this word Artificial Intelligence?” i expected a wide range of responses but you know what messages i received instead? Yeah you are right, robots taking over the world, there will be no more job security e.t.c. I got a bunch of fears not a single comment was about the bright side. This got me wondering maybe AI will take over for sure or maybe it will not. Despite all those theories we read about daily about the AI apocalypse or those fears we think of, i found out that having those worries today is an advantage if we are to narrow down with the proposed dark side because we will take precautions and establish a failsafe. But there is always another side of the coin, so should we really focus with only one side of it? The answer is no, so why have we labeled AI as a time bomb? Well it doesn’t matter lets see the time left on it to blow up.

The impact on jobs

When it comes to jobs and the future everyone is not really satisfied that the present jobs will still be available or even will there be jobs that AI will not be able to accomplish so that humans can take those job positions. But it is obvious that AI will change the human workforce and the nature of work itself. This change is what led people to perceive that the robots will take all of our jobs and gain control over humanity, but the truth that is certain is that the job market will definitely change, and we are seeing signs of this already, for instance :

In the transport sector, the introduction of autonomous vehicles powered by AI is helping in the management of traffic-flows and design of
transport systems and we are seeing that there is a decrease in demand for certain driving jobs.

In education, AI technologies are supporting personalised learning systems and more are more students are shifting to these technologies for better, fast and precise studying routines, which means students can homeschool their selves without going to school or the library everyday.

In healthcare, AI is enabling new diagnostic and decision-support tools for medical professionals and also it is creating an environment to practice precision medicine where patients can be in charge of their health while on the go, lessening their hospital visits or doctor appointments.

In manufacturing programmed machines (robots) are being used to tackle bulk, repetitive and unsafe work loads and they are doing this fast and more accurate. In manufacturing AI is considered to be useful and a super substitute for the human workforce.

All in all, across all sectors today, artificial intelligence is being put to use to analyse huge quantities of data, in improving business services and designing new ones. There are various AI applications being developed at different speeds, and their adoption among businesses is variable.But much of AI’s promise on the future of jobs is making as many jobs as possible easier. What we might see besides our jobs being taken is the reconfiguration of roles as some of the tasks become displaced and some being created.

So in short AI will simply reshape the kinds of jobs that are available today thus creating new ones and also in many cases, these new jobs will be more engaging than the repetitive tasks of the past.

The impact of AI on economies and employment (working culture)

With a huge number of jobs that
Will be affected by AI, there will be also an impact on the economies and the structure of employment. That is the advent of AI will sweep off the present cultures at the workplaces and this will give rise to new routines or ways of working culture and new approaches that will reshape the economies of the countries and the world at large.

The displacement and creation of new opportunities influences the way in which social, economic, and technical systems combine. This will affect different groups in different ways due to the time-lapse between the adoption of new technology and
its positive impacts among different groups.

However, when AI affects jobs certainly it is most of the labour that will be is replaced and this will be counteracted by other effects including:

I) INCREASED PRODUCTIVITY :

Goods and services will become
cheaper and better, stimulating additional demand for the products and thus the labour required to produce more, thus deploying more machines for more production.

II) CAPITAL ACCUMULATION :

As more machines are deployed, driving down labour costs , there will be enhancement of productivity resulting in improved economies.

According to the BRITISH ACADEMY , the most disruptive technologies will have more positive long-term impacts on work as they create the strongest productivity, new jobs and positive capital accumulation .

However, some applications of AI will simply have a substitution effect without creating sufficient productivity gains on the economy.

CONCLUSION :HUMANS VS AI

Yes artificial intelligence is smart, and it keeps on evolving, that is what got many of us worried. We don’t want to be outsmarted.But because AI is smart, it makes a great tool that helps people accomplish every complex task, that we humans can’t do in our own ways . If we implement AI in a way that we can work side to side together, we will make the world a better place. We will be able to create strong and economic car engines, design aerodynamic plane wings, predict economic changes,solve climate change problems, perform surgeries, and detect cancer and many other awesome things.

We do not have to be afraid of super AI, or any form of AI that they maybe one day be able to take control because their essence is completely dependent on the developers designing them. They will need to take some time to achieve the ability of abstract thinking, that is it will take some time until it will (maybe) be able to think on its own. Today, all of the algorithms present are developed with the ability of doing discrete operations much faster than a human can do and that is not something new at all. For instance we have used calculators to solve mathematical problems for some time and because they are able to solve those problems faster than we can, it hasn’t mean that they are smarter, or wiser than us.

AI is no more than a powerful tool because they will never be independent of humans due to their descrete way of solving problems. They will never do something because they want to, instead AI will only do that what it is programmed to do, and nothing else.

Alexandru Tornea once said this

AI is just a tool in a human’s hands and any tool can be either helpful or harmful, depending on who’s using it.

So as we move towards the future, it is important that we not only understand the potential advantages or disadvantages of adopting AI, but also help to shape them. Investing in the technology cannot be done solo. Instead, every one must find purpose in crafting of robust strategies that take account of the entire future changes.

It is certain that in AI will improve greatly and will change most of the things if not everything present. We have seen, we are seeing AI being very useful, and it will be in the future,with more and more benefits, but instead of focusing on the dark side of AI which is not even here, let us work hard on developing ethical algorithms with good quality control.This is because with the right combination of capabilities and resources joined together alongside the right mind-set, miracles will happen!

Artificial Intelligence (Examples & types)

June 17, 2019 Tinotenda JoeLeave a comment

The essence of Artificial Intelligence narrows down to how close or how well a computer can imitate or go beyond, when compared to human being.

AI is undoubtedly multifaceted, there are specific types of artificial intelligence under which extended categories fall and also there are two types of main classification:

TYPE 1

a) Weak artificial intelligence

Weak artificial intelligence is also known as narrow artificial. It is a form of AI specifically designed to be focused on a narrow task and to seem very intelligent at it. Weak AI is never taken as a general intelligence but rather a construct designed to be intelligent in the narrow task that it is assigned to.

This is an approach to artificial intelligence research and development with the consideration that AI is and will always be a simulation of human cognitive function, and that computers can only appear to think but are not actually conscious in any sense of the word. Weak AI simply acts upon and is bound by the rules imposed on it and it could not go beyond those rules. A good example of weak AI are characters in a computer game that act believably within the context of their game character, but are unable to do anything beyond that.

A very good example of weak AI is Apple’s Siri, which has the Internet behind it serving as a powerful database. Siri seems very intelligent, as it is able to hold a conversation with actual people, even giving snide remarks and a few jokes, but actually operates in a very narrow, predefined manner. However, the “narrowness” of its function can be evidenced by its inaccurate results when it is engaged in conversations that it is not programmed to respond to

b)Strong artificial intelligence
Strong artificial intelligence also known as full intelligence is more of a philosophy rather than an actual approach in creating AI. It is a different perception of AI wherein it equates AI to humans. It stipulates that a computer can be programmed to actually be a human mind, to be intelligent in every sense of the word, to have perception, beliefs and have other cognitive states that are normally only ascribed to humans

The construct of strong artificial intelligence has mental capabilities and functions that mimic the human brain. In the philosophy of strong AI, there is no essential difference between the piece of software, which is the AI, exactly emulating the actions of the human brain, and actions of a human being, including its power of understanding and even its consciousness.

TYPE 2 (classification based on functionality)

a) Reactive Machines:

This is one of the basic forms of AI. It doesn’t have past memory and cannot use past information to influence present made decisions or future actions.

Reactive machines have no concept of the world and therefore cannot function beyond the simple tasks for which they are programmed. A characteristic of reactive machines is that no matter the time or place, these machines will always behave the way they were programmed. There is no growth with reactive machines, only stagnation in recurring actions and behaviors. To simplify, reactive machines are programmed for the here and now, but not the before and after.
An existing form of a reactive machine is Deep Blue, a chess-playing supercomputer created by IBM in the mid-1980s.

b) Limited Memory:

Basically this AI is comprised of machine learning models that derive knowledge from previously-learned information, stored data, or events. Unlike reactive machines, limited memory learns from the past by observing actions or data fed to them in order to build experiential knowledge.

Although limited memory builds on observational data in conjunction with pre-programmed data the machines already contain, these sample pieces of information are fleeting. An existing form of limited memory is autonomous vehicles.

Some of the decision-making functions in self-driving cars have been designed this way. Observations used to inform actions happening in the not so distant future, such as the car that has changed lanes, their environment, detect patterns or changes in external factors, and adjust as necessary.

C) Theory of Mind:

This type of AI should be able to understand people’s emotion, belief, thoughts, expectations and be able to interact socially Even though a lot of improvements are there in this field this kind of AI is not complete yet.
What constitutes theory of mind is decision-making ability equal to the extent of a human mind, but by machines. While there are some machines that currently exhibit humanlike capabilities (voice assistants, for instance), none are fully capable of holding conversations relative to human standards. One component of human conversation is having emotional capacity, or sounding and behaving like a person would in standard conventions of conversation.

This future class of machine ability would include understanding that people have thoughts and emotions that affect behavioral output and thus influence a “theory of mind” machine’s thought process. Social interaction is a key facet of human interaction, so to make theory of mind machines tangible, the AI systems that control the now-hypothetical machines would have to identify, understand, retain, and remember emotional output and behaviors while knowing how to respond to them.

From this, said theory of mind machines would have to be able to use the information derived from people and adapt it into their learning centers to know how to communicate with and treat different situations. Theory of mind is a highly advanced form of proposed artificial intelligence that would require machines to thoroughly acknowledge rapid shifts in emotional and behavioral patterns in humans, and also understand that human behavior is fluid; thus, theory of mind machines would have to be able to learn rapidly at a moment’s notice.

d) Self-aware AI

This involves AI that has it’s own conscious, super intelligent, self-awareness and sentient (In simple words a complete human being). Of course, this kind of intelligent doesn’t exist yet but if achieved it will be one of the milestones in the field of AI.

Aspects of self-aware AI include the ability to not only recognize and replicate humanlike actions, but also to think for itself, have desires, and understand its feelings. Self-aware AI, in essence, is an advancement and extension of theory of mind AI. Where theory of mind only focuses on the aspects of comprehension and replication of human practices, self-aware AI takes it a step further by implying that it can and will have self-guided thoughts and reactions.

Today we are presently in a relative position where three of the four type 2 classification of artificial intelligence have been archived, so believing that we could potentially reach the fourth (and final?) level of AI doesn’t seem like a far-fetched idea.

But for now, it’s important to focus on perfecting all aspects of types two in AI. Sloppily speeding through each AI level could be detrimental to the future of artificial intelligence for generations to come.

Despite of the challenges along the applications of AI, we should not stop trying to attain the the best with AI. It must go beyond the capabilities of humans for it to reach its peak potential

Artificial Intelligence (mini-introduction)

June 15, 2019June 16, 2019 Tinotenda JoeLeave a comment

Artificial intelligence (AI) is an area of computer science that emphasizes the
creation of intelligent machines that work and react like humans. Some of the activities computers with artificial intelligence are designed for include: Speech recognition,Learning, Planning, Problem solving. AI is increasing every day and since it was introduced to the market, it has be a reason of the quick change in technology and business fields.

Under this topic i shall briefly talk about the following subjects; history of Ai, Deep learning, Machine learning, the pros and cons of artificial intelligence.

History of Artificial Intelligence:
Artificial Intelligence was first proposed by John McCarthy in 1956 in his first academic conference on the subject. The idea of machines operating like human beings began to be the center of scientist’s mind and whether if it is possible to make machines have the same ability to
think and learn by itself was introduced by the mathematician Alan Turing. Alan Turing was able
to put his hypotheses and questions into actions by testing whether “machines can think”? After
series of testing (later was called as Turing Test) it turns out that it is possible to enable machines
to think and learn just like humans. Turing Test uses the pragmatic approach to be able to identify if machines can respond as humans.

Deep Learning

Deep learning has enabled many practical applications of Machine Learning and
by extension the overall field of AI. Deep Learning breaks down tasks in ways that makes all kinds of machine assists seem possible, even likely. Driverless cars, better preventive
healthcare, even better movie recommendations, are all here today and more are on the horizon.
AI i s the present and the future. With Deep Learning’s help, AI may even get to that science fiction state we’ve so long imagined.

Machine Learning

Machine Learning at its most basic isthe practice of using algorithms to parse
data, learn from it, and then make a determination or prediction about something in the world. So rather than hand-coding software routines with a specific set of instructions to accomplish a particular task, the machine is “trained” using large
amounts of data and algorithmsthat give it the ability to learn how to perform the task.

Pros And Cons Of AI

AI offers reliability, cost-effectiveness, solve complicated problems, and make decisions; in addition, AI restrict data from getting lost. AI is applied nowadays in most fields
whether business or engineering. One of the great tools in AI is called “reinforcement learning”
which is based on testing success and failure in real life to increase the reliability of applications. Unfortunately, AI is limited with its capability and functionality.

Although Artificial Intelligence made our lives much easier and saved us more time than
ever, scientists are predicting that by the huge dependency on AI humanity could extinct. Scientists also argue that by having a AI machines, people will be jobless and that will conclude in losing the sense of living. Since machines are learning and doing things more efficiently and
effectively in a timely manner, could this be the reason of our extinction?
