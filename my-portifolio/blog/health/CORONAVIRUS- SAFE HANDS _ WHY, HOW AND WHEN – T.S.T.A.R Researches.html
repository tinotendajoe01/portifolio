CORONAVIRUS- SAFE HANDS : WHY, HOW AND WHEN

March 29, 2020March 29, 2020 Tinotenda Joe


Practicing extreme hygiene has always been an active measure to fight diseases for decades now. We have been told to wash our hands our whole life, from childhood to adulthood, but lately hand hygiene became a serious thing of concern, and the awareness of safe hands is spreading as fast as the infectious (coronavirus) disease it is trying to prevent. #safehands has become the new hype of this fresh decade.

In this midst of the Corona virus pandemic, the world is encouraging each and every one to actively engage in this challenge so as to minimize the risk of contracting and spreading the virus, but it is surprisingly that one out of 5 people do not wash their hands and of those who wash only one out of three uses detergents. Analyzed statistics from WATERAID reviewed that a larger proportion do not wash their hands because they are ignorant,the others is because they have no access to water and the proper detergents. This blog post serves to address those who are ignorant and do not know the “how to” part.

As outlined by W. H. O, it is our responsibility to obverse the Ignaz Semmelweis antiseptic method of washing hands. However a large proportion does not know how to actually wash their hands the right way. Here we provide you with the approved hand washing methodology.
What most people do not know is that hands are responsible for the spread of 80% of common infectious diseases and thoroughly washing your hands is the easiest and cheapest way to prevent the spread of viruses and bacteria. And if keeping our hands clean is key in preventing getting sick , there is need for more people to do it, and do it right.


Before this year, thorough washing of hands used to be a thing observed in health-care workers, caregiver but with the wide spread of the corona virus, the whole population whether or not you are involved in direct or indirect interaction with different people, hand hygiene has become a significant protocol and everyone should be able to perform it correctly and at the right time. With so many sources of information, there are many different recommended hand washing methods and guidelines, one might find it difficult to know exactly which hand washing method is the most effective. Here is the most approved technique from the World Health Organisation (WHO). A 6-step hand washing technique that is more effective at removing bacteria and other condemnations than the simple YouTube wash your hands for 20 seconds methods.

Use the following steps when washing your hands thoroughly and properly during the pandemic:

Step 1: Wet hands with water
Step 2: Apply single drop of detergent
Step 3: Rub hands palm to palm
Step 4: Rub back of each hand with the palm of other the hand with fingers interlaced
Step 5: Rub palm to palm with fingers interlaced
Step 6: Rub with backs of fingers to opposing palms with fingers interlocked
Step 7: Rub each thumb clasped in opposite hand using rotational movement
Step 8 :Rub tips of fingers in opposite palm in circular motion
Step 9 : Rub each wrist with opposite hand
Step 10 :Rinse hands with water and dry thoroughly.

Adopting to a good hand washing method is urgent to ensure hands are thoroughly clean. Focus and attention should be paid to the backs of the hands and fingertips as these are the frequently missed points.

It is certain that water will not be always available at different places, in such cases, alcohol based hand sanitizers are recommend since they immediately kill the bacteria.

Today, possessing the knowledge on how to wash your hands the right way is an important step in preventing the spread of corona virus wherever your are. Most important is knowing when to wash your hands. The following highlights crucial points where one must actively wash their hands.

  • a) At the end of each hand on activity.
  • b) Before each break
  • c) Before eating or preparing food
  • d) After going to the restroom
  • e) Whenever hands are dirty or contaminated



Healthcare workers must follow a stricter hand washing process, using the “The 5 Moments of Hand Hygiene” as recommended by WHO:

  • Before touching a patient,
  • Before clean/aseptic procedures,
  • After body fluid exposure/risk,
  • After touching a patient, and
  • -After touching patient surroundings

Another important issue is the access to clean water. Without clean running water individuals will not be able to practice proper hands hygiene and they will not be safe from viruses such as the Corona virus. Health workers will not be protected from infections, and lives will not be saved in hospitals. Safe water, sanitation and hygiene are the basics of health. And to prevent disease like corona virus from spreading, cities need to redouble their efforts to make sure people have access to safe water and the people must not waste water.
