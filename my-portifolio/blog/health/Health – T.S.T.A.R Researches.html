PHARMACY: PROFITABLE VENTURES

April 29, 2020April 29, 2020 Tinotenda Joe

It is quite surprising that today over 90% of pharmacists are bored with their jobs. The traditional job as a pharmacist can be described as extremely monotonous and because of this many pharmacists are searching for alternative careers or business ideas for them to lighten up their lives.

I am a pharmacy student and i am not surprised that most people including some pharmacists and pharmacy students think that being a pharmacist starts and ends being a drug dispenser at a pharmacy, hospital or owning a drug store. While they are not wrong, I would like to think that those are just some of the things pharmacists do.

The reality is Pharmacists do not solely dispense drugs, they are involved in researching and developing new drugs to control and eliminate new and existing diseases, advising doctors on drug recommendations and dosages, they counsel patients and do a whole lot of other things.

The public may not agree with this but the truth is that without a pharmacist, the job of a doctor, dentist, ophthalmologist or even a surgeon is incomplete. All medical professionals, regardless of their field need pharmacists for support and advice when it comes to drug related issues.

Well, here is my message to my fellow pharmacists, the job of a pharmacist doesn’t have to be monotonous and boring and as a pharmacist, you really do not have to be confined to dispensing drugs to patients. There are several other business opportunities you could explore which would not only make your career more interesting, but also earn you more money.

Here is a list of opportunities the profession offers.

a) Drug Marketing

  • If you are a good sales person, you could think about becoming a drug marketer, especially if you are someone who enjoys commerce. As a drug marketer, you could make money from selling and distributing drugs made by different pharmaceutical companies to hospitals and pharmacies. You could choose to do this as an representative of a single drug company or as an independent marketer selling to different companies.

b) 24-hour Pharmacy

  • People can get sick at any time and having access to drugs at every hour of the day could be a life saver for many people with medical emergencies. However, to start this kind of business, you would need tight security and staffs that can run shifts to keep the pharmacy running hitch-free 24/7.

c) Mobile Pharmacy

  • A lot of sick people usually do not find it in their interest to move up and down buying their prescription medications. It would be a lucrative business venture to carry out a doorstep delivery scheme.

d) Drug Research and Development .

  • Since pharmacists are responsible for researching and developing drugs. This means that as a pharmacist, you could start your own drug manufacturing business. If you do not have enough capital, to purchase the facilities and equipment required to produce your own drugs, you could do all the research and development and then outsource the actual manufacturing of the drugs to drug manufacturing companies.

e) Medical writer

  • If you are into content creation, you could pursue a career as a medical writer, helping online healthcare platforms to write contents about drugs, uses and side effects. You can also start your own platform providing useful information and then monetize the platform.

f) Medical Counseling

  • Patients suffering from terminal and life threatening health issues are usually referred to medical counselors to help them and their families understand the situation and help them to understand what they should be doing to make the situation a lot better.

g) Cosmetics line

  • The cosmetics industry is also growing and you can invest in it too by starting your own cosmetics line, producing and selling products like body creams, hair creams, perfumes, body soaps and anti-aging products.

h) Consultancy Services.

  • We both kniw that other medical practitioners need the support of pharmacists to perform their jobs. This is because pharmacists understand drugs better, it is their field of specialty, so they are in the best position to advice other medical experts on the kinds of drugs to recommend for specific cases and also make them understand the side effects of those drugs and how they could affect patients with specific cases. As a pharmacist, you could become an independent consultant, offering your services to other medical professionals for a fee


i) Pharmaceutical Benefit Management

  • Pharmaceutical benefit management companies help to mediate between patients and health insurance management companies on drug benefits and values. This is a business oriented venture for pharmacist who are interested in business.


j) Device And Apps Developing & Marketing

  • If you’re interested in technology and it’s applications in healthcare as a pharmacists you can branch out from selling drugs and venture into sales and distribution of medical devices instead. Another option to consider is designing and selling mobile applications to teach people about drugs and their side effects. You could also design an application to remind people to take their prescriptions. You can do a lot in this venture

h) Online Pharmacy

  • This is one of my favorite, many people fail to establish their own brick and mortar pharmacy buildings and stop there and then but today the reality is you don’t necessarily need a physical store to sell your drugs. You could consider starting an online pharmacy but that is after you must have checked with the regulatory authorities in your country to be sure it is not illegal in your country to sell prescription drugs online.

i) Alternative medicine .

  • More people are embracing herbal medicine these days. You could start a pharmacy that sells only herbal drugs or even develop your own herbal drugs. The resources here are cheaper and easily accessible.

The opportunities can not be exhausted, there is more. All it takes is your dedication and knowing what you want to achieve and where you want to be as a pharmacist.

CORONAVIRUS- SAFE HANDS : WHY, HOW AND WHEN

March 29, 2020March 29, 2020 Tinotenda Joe


Practicing extreme hygiene has always been an active measure to fight diseases for decades now. We have been told to wash our hands our whole life, from childhood to adulthood, but lately hand hygiene became a serious thing of concern, and the awareness of safe hands is spreading as fast as the infectious (coronavirus) disease it is trying to prevent. #safehands has become the new hype of this fresh decade.

In this midst of the Corona virus pandemic, the world is encouraging each and every one to actively engage in this challenge so as to minimize the risk of contracting and spreading the virus, but it is surprisingly that one out of 5 people do not wash their hands and of those who wash only one out of three uses detergents. Analyzed statistics from WATERAID reviewed that a larger proportion do not wash their hands because they are ignorant,the others is because they have no access to water and the proper detergents. This blog post serves to address those who are ignorant and do not know the “how to” part.

As outlined by W. H. O, it is our responsibility to obverse the Ignaz Semmelweis antiseptic method of washing hands. However a large proportion does not know how to actually wash their hands the right way. Here we provide you with the approved hand washing methodology.
What most people do not know is that hands are responsible for the spread of 80% of common infectious diseases and thoroughly washing your hands is the easiest and cheapest way to prevent the spread of viruses and bacteria. And if keeping our hands clean is key in preventing getting sick , there is need for more people to do it, and do it right.


Before this year, thorough washing of hands used to be a thing observed in health-care workers, caregiver but with the wide spread of the corona virus, the whole population whether or not you are involved in direct or indirect interaction with different people, hand hygiene has become a significant protocol and everyone should be able to perform it correctly and at the right time. With so many sources of information, there are many different recommended hand washing methods and guidelines, one might find it difficult to know exactly which hand washing method is the most effective. Here is the most approved technique from the World Health Organisation (WHO). A 6-step hand washing technique that is more effective at removing bacteria and other condemnations than the simple YouTube wash your hands for 20 seconds methods.

Use the following steps when washing your hands thoroughly and properly during the pandemic:

Step 1: Wet hands with water
Step 2: Apply single drop of detergent
Step 3: Rub hands palm to palm
Step 4: Rub back of each hand with the palm of other the hand with fingers interlaced
Step 5: Rub palm to palm with fingers interlaced
Step 6: Rub with backs of fingers to opposing palms with fingers interlocked
Step 7: Rub each thumb clasped in opposite hand using rotational movement
Step 8 :Rub tips of fingers in opposite palm in circular motion
Step 9 : Rub each wrist with opposite hand
Step 10 :Rinse hands with water and dry thoroughly.

Adopting to a good hand washing method is urgent to ensure hands are thoroughly clean. Focus and attention should be paid to the backs of the hands and fingertips as these are the frequently missed points.

It is certain that water will not be always available at different places, in such cases, alcohol based hand sanitizers are recommend since they immediately kill the bacteria.

Today, possessing the knowledge on how to wash your hands the right way is an important step in preventing the spread of corona virus wherever your are. Most important is knowing when to wash your hands. The following highlights crucial points where one must actively wash their hands.

  • a) At the end of each hand on activity.
  • b) Before each break
  • c) Before eating or preparing food
  • d) After going to the restroom
  • e) Whenever hands are dirty or contaminated



Healthcare workers must follow a stricter hand washing process, using the “The 5 Moments of Hand Hygiene” as recommended by WHO:

  • Before touching a patient,
  • Before clean/aseptic procedures,
  • After body fluid exposure/risk,
  • After touching a patient, and
  • -After touching patient surroundings

Another important issue is the access to clean water. Without clean running water individuals will not be able to practice proper hands hygiene and they will not be safe from viruses such as the Corona virus. Health workers will not be protected from infections, and lives will not be saved in hospitals. Safe water, sanitation and hygiene are the basics of health. And to prevent disease like corona virus from spreading, cities need to redouble their efforts to make sure people have access to safe water and the people must not waste water.

Capping costs and encouraging competition increases drugs affordability!

February 22, 2020February 22, 2020 Tinotenda Joe

A lot of developing countries are in huge pharmaceutical debts, prices for medicines are sky rocketing. With these high prescription drug prices, it is of no doubt that people on prescription drugs and seniors taking drugs are finding it difficult for them to afford their medications. On avarage 1 out of 3 patients will afford to to pay up their prescription medications and this is so bad

Nearly 50% of adults are on prescription medications and the majority of which pay up the funds with money out of their pockets. With little monthly income, these individuals will be trying to cut costs in any way possible. That is by seeking for a cheaper alternative drug, not taking medicine as prescribed, or seeking alternative therapies. To worsen things, a large proportion of these people do not have medical insurance and all they can do is “save” money for the next prescription.

Since the governments are the ones responsible for the availability and pricing of medicines, they are deemed responsible for the numbers of patients failing to afford their medications. In poor countries, governments have the tendency of issuing fixed amounts of money that will be forked out to source medicines and lay back. This is the first mistake in the process despite the fact that they are already incapacitated to source enough drugs.

To solve the problems the government must actively intervene and dictate prices. The government as a big purchaser of pharmaceuticals must insist on the inclusion of big discounts in medical insurances. This can be done through fruitful negotiations of lowering drug prices on medical insurance premiums. If associated with the manufacturers, the drug companies will be able to provide higher rebates for prescription drugs to low-income patients on insurances in the long run.
The insurance companies must also be urged to work together towards lowering of pharmaceutical prices, this practice is difficult to implement when competition prevails but it can be implemented. If the insurance companies agree upon the practice for instance, they can purchase at least 3 drugs in each of the categories of medications such as parenterals, oral contraceptives, or oral diabetic agents. Then they will standing on advantageous competing grounds relative to rival, monopolized pharmaceutical companies. The latter will be forced to drop their prices.

Visible pricing is also another thing developing countries are not prioritizing. The prevalence of price differential is contributing to the rise of expensive medications.
Imagine the possibilities if each provider and pharmacy had to publish online the average price as well as the lowest and highest price for which they sell each drug entity,what will be the end result? Visibility of information towards drug prices will mean that there will be transparency and unreasonable prices will drop down.

Other minor but worthwhile attempts includes:

  • negotiating with healthcare providers, and pharmaceuticals, to keep costs under control
  • prioritizing subsidiary research of drugs for orphan diseases and if it is not profitable enough for the free market to care about
  • placing much emphasis towards preventive care to avoid more expensive curative care
  • educating the populace on how to best care for themselves, and reducing unnecessary or marginally useful medical services.
  • treating healthcare as a common good and not like a consumer product.

What are herbs?

January 23, 2020January 23, 2020 Tinotenda Joe

Medicine is in your garden! Medicine is in your park! Medicine is within your reach!.

Herbs are those usable parts of herbaceous plants ( a plant that lacks a woody stem). The word “herb” most often refers to plants that have culinary, cosmetic, or medicinal uses and the culinary uses of herbs are what differentiates them from spices. Often times people confuse herbs and spices but simply defined from Oxford dictionaries: herbs refers to the leafy green or flowering parts of a plant, fresh or dried, spices, on the other hand, are produced from the other parts of the plants, which are usually dried, including berries, roots, barks, seeds, and fruits.

So in essence, herbs are plants and most of the herbs poses healing, preservative and fragrant or aromatic properties. This synergy of the herbs employs them for use in various areas as listed below.

  • In food industry
  • In pharmaceutical industry
  • In manufacture of cosmetic products
  • In decorating houses

To mention a few, Basil, parsley, rosemary, thyme, and dill are all herbs and note that for each of these, the important part is the green or leafy part of some kind of plant. In the case of basil, the leaves can be quite large, whereas rosemary leaves are more like spines of an evergreen plant.

Despite the mentioned uses, it is their healing and restorative power that largely contribute to their importance. Though the healing power of herbs is grossly underestimated, today we don’t doubt that fresh fruits and green vegetables, seeds and nuts contain a wide range of vitamins, minerals and some cancer-fighting properties, yet the nutrient content and medicinal properties of herbs are often overlooked.

With that in mind, we should therefore see herbs as the nature’s prescription for the ills and disease that affect us. We are embedded with nature, and as we noticed earlier, the symbiotic relationship has provided us with this rich source of healing in our own environment. In herbs lies the powerful secret that the pharmaceutical industry is taping into somw of their artificial medicinal products. It is the Twenty-First Century, there is modernization of every.. But it is important and beneficial to us if we notice that medicine has always been growing around us.

Herbal Chemistry

January 23, 2020January 23, 2020 Tinotenda Joe

The Origins of Herbal Medicine :

Archeology has it that from the beginning of our formation we been living in a symbiotic alliance with the environment and we been using the ingredients of the environment to obtain food and medicine for quite a long time. Dating 500 B.C it is noted that the ancient Chinese, Indians, Egyptians, Babylonians, and Native Americans were all herbalists. The oldest known list of Medicinal herbs is Shen Nung’s Pen Ts’ao or Shennong Ben Cao Jing (c. 3000 B.C.), a Chinese herbal that is probably a compilation of an even older oral tradition.
The ancient Greeks and Romans were also renowned herbalists. Surgeons traveling with the Roman army spread their herbal expertise throughout the Roman empire, in Spain, Germany, France, and England. Dioscorides (c. 40-c. 90) and Galen (131-200 A.D.), both Greek surgeons in the Roman army, compiled herbals that remained the definitive materia medica texts for 1500 years.-from “Herbs Friend Of Physician, Praise Of Cook“

The origin of herbalism is finely looped with that of medicine, that is from ancient times of the germ theory of disease in the 19th century up until the revolution of modern medicine. From the 19th century to date, the chemistry of medicines has been based on evidence collected using various scientific methods. Today you might have heard of the phrase “evidence-based” in healthcare practices. The same applys in the Pharmacy industry and the phrase represents use of pharmaceutical drugs, often derived from medicinal plants that has previously proved to possess healing capabilities. However, the morden practice has largely replaced pure herbal treatments though a small proportion of people continue to employ various forms of traditional healing chemistry. These small proportions traditional healing systems often have a significant herbal component. The history of herbalism also overlaps with that of food, as many of the herbs and spices were used by humans to season food yield useful medicinal compounds, and use of spices with antimicrobial activity in cooking is part of an ancient response to the threat of food-borne pathogens.

While traditional medicine is “the knowledge, skills and practices based on the theories, beliefs and experiences indigenous to different cultures, used in the maintenance of health and in the prevention, diagnosis, improvement or treatment of physical and mental illness” ( World Health Organization:Traditional meds ), It is therefore important not to forfeit herbalism despite its dilution with the pharmaceutical industry. Since the main focus is on the overall well-being of the individual, rather than on the particular complaint or disease from which an individual is suffering, the use of herbs is a core part of pharmacy and medicine.

Pharmaceuticals And Developing Countries (Conclusion)

August 16, 2019August 17, 2019 Tinotenda JoeLeave a comment

Here is a brief conclusion of my episodes on pharmaceuticals and developing countries.

Bigpharma have already brought tremendous health improvements to developing countries, despite of the challenges that these countries internationally face. The International community could greatly increase these benefits by implementing systems to provide better access to the existing pharmaceuticals and to manage their use as well by investing in the global public good of pharmaceutical R&D on diseases thay disproportionately affects the poor.

Developing counties must also redirect their health budgets away from salaries towards cost effective public health measures such as vaccinations and curative disease control methods. They also have to explore institutional reforms for proper health care delivery,and encouragement of differential pricing. The latter allow more favorable tax treatment of appropriate drug donations and also encourage R&D.

Drug Regulation And Procurement

August 16, 2019August 16, 2019 Tinotenda Joe1 Comment

Gathering information on drug safety and efficiency is an international public good. I am sure there is a role for an international body that reviews developed countries pharmaceuticals’ approval decisions for relevance to developing countries conditions and where appropriate to sponsor additional R&D trials or issues certification. More like regulation.

The justification for pharmaceuticals regulation need to be reconceptualized frequently. Basically the declared purpose of pharmaceuticals regulation as you already know is to protect consumers from unsafe and ineffective drugs. While the primary advantage is that it creates incentives for firms to conduct the randomized trials that provide information on product effectiveness fot future consumers.

The current regulatory system, in which the products that have not undergone clinical trials cannot be sold legally, gives pharmaceutical firms an incentive to conduct these trials and to do so in a rigorous way to pass through these regulations. Seen in this light some consider the regulations a hindrance to the current consumer since it denies the option to take unproven drugs, but it provides future consumers with information about the drugs and its use.

Some say the traditional justification for drug regulation may better apply in environments where consumers are often illiterate for regulation of unproved drugs can be distorted by deceptive advertising which is also difficult to control in an environment where tort law is weak.

Others suggest that labeling can solve the challenges brought forward by the regulations, but replacing prohibition with labeling could potentially exacerbate misuse ofpharmaceuticals.

As a way of improving pharmaceuticals procurement in developing countries, posting information on all public pharmaceutical purchases including the internet. Also the act of public posting of prices could reduce corruption in drug procurement.
